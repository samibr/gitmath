Convergence of Sequences Given a Quadratic Sum Condition

Let $(u_n)$ and $(v_n)$ be two real sequences such that

$$ u_n^2+ u_n v_n + v_n^2 \xrightarrow[n\to+\infty] {}0.$$

Prove that the sequences $(u_n)$ and $(v_n)$ converge to 0.

Show Answer:
We have $$0\le (u_n+v_n)^2= u_n^2+2u_n v_n+ v_n^2\le 2(u_n^2+u_n v_n+ v_n^2)\xrightarrow[n\to+\infty]{}0.$$ Hence $u_n+v_n \xrightarrow[n\to+\infty]{}0$. Also $$u_n v_n= (u_n+v_n)^2 -(u_n^2+u_n v_n+ v_n^2)\xrightarrow[n\to+\infty]{}0$$ so $$u_n^2+v_n^2= 2(u_n^2+u_n v_n+ v_n^2)-(u_n+v_n)^2\xrightarrow[n\to+\infty]{}0$$ which allows to conclude that $u_n\xrightarrow[n\to+\infty]{}0$ and $v_n\xrightarrow[n\to+\infty]{}0$.
